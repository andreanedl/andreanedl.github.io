Control


Watering

The first attempt was to use an air pump to create pressure in a plastic bottle which pushes the water out of it. I used an extra pump I had from a previous project. For ShotBot, it's very important to use an air pump and not a suction one to keep the soda carbonated and not spill soda everywhere. In this case, it was creating a syphon since the exit is lower than the water level in the bottle. For Smart Garden, the pump would turn off, but the water would keep flowing until the water level is underneath the water exit. I finally replaced the air pump by a suction pump and it works perfectly now.

Ventilation

At first, I had a sensor, but nothing to control the humidity yet. The first readings were at 95% and my sister told me it should be at 85%. I tried to just open the lid a little bit, but it dropped the humidity down to 75%. I finally added a fan (very quiet!) and a simple on-off controller using a relay to fix the problem.

Full Spectrum Light

For the light, I got a full spectrum light bulb at Home Depot. It's made for that, so of couse it works well. It's turned on and off using a relay.
