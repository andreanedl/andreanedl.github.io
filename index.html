My Projects


ShotBot

I decided to make a robotic bartender and 1 week later, it was working. Since then, I've added some features like the screen and the RFID identification system. The random function seems to be the most popular one, you get a random alcohol with a random soda which can result in a pretty weird mix.

Woodworking

I've always wanted to make a Wood CNC Router so when Richard-Alexandre Peloquin told me he wanted to make one too, we started working on the budget the same day. The machine was fun to make and has been very useful. I also took a woodworking class at Allied Woodshop which reminded me how convinient the CNC is, but also made me respect traditional woodworkers even more.

Mini Bikes

This project started with Raphaël Pilon making a mini bike. Maxime Leboeuf, Richard-Alexandre Peloquin and I got excited about it so we started to help him. Making the mini bike was the first step, but we had to test it so Maxime invited us to his family's farm for a weekend. He has a lot of land and a wood, so it was perfect. The testing weekend was such a success that we decided to make more vehicles and do it again.

Smart Garden

I don't have a garden yet, so I've decided to make an indoor one. I knew what I was doing when I automated the functions, the Smart part of Smart Garden wasn't the problem, the Garden part was. I was lost and scared of messing up, so I got some advice from my sister and my mom. They helped me choose what I would plant. I wanted to grow cucumbers, but it's probably too big/tall. We finally decided to grow some arugula and spinach. This project was very interesting, unfortunately mother nature won this battle. It's not all lost, I can apply what I've learn to other robotics project. Next time, I'll try to keep living things out of my projects.

Glass Blowing

I've only taken 3 classes at KT Glassworks, so I'm a beginner. Actually, I don't think beginner is the right term in that case because I needed assistance from the teacher at all time. It's the hardest thing I've ever tried for many reasons, but the main one is that time is part of the equation. You can't just slow down or take a break to think about the next steps. If you do, the glass is going to fall down. You also have to warm up your part often so it doesn't cool down too much and break. It's very hard, but I liked it a lot. I recommand this experience.

Cooking

I wish I could say I don't like cooking to break the stereotype, but I do. I see cooking the same way as an engineering project. The design is as important as the fabrication. You have to think about what's the best way to do it so it tastes good, but also how you could make it as easy as possible to make. I like the challenge of cooking efficiently: good, fast, and minimum cleaning. I create my own recipes unless the quantities are very important like for baking. In my opinion, Ricardo's recipes are the best.
